National Sampling Frame Dashboard

A sampling frame is a population from which a sample can be drawn. It serves as the source material that ensures survey samples are representative, accurate, and reliable. A well-maintained sampling frame is essential for statistical operations, as it determines the quality of survey results and the credibility of official statistics.

The National Statistics Bureau (NSB) maintains a sampling frame comprising 3,310 Enumeration Areas (EAs), disaggregated into 1,464 urban and 1,846 rural developed from the Population and Housing Census of Bhutan (PHCB) 2017. Each EA has a corresponding digital map and associated metadata.
